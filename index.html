Narcosis

Pain can be the ink, but too much of it burns the paper, My pen has dried up — the ink within it, rather My mind has become a rock — a dark hole, a dry well My poet lies unconscious, how to wake her?
The colour of life has been rinsed from my skin My wounds are too stubborn to let words through My mouth is too busy screaming to sing any sad songs My poet lies unconscious, how to wake her?
Gravity is pulling harder on me than anyone else, My eyes are blinking too fast — my tears are all I can see My legs are waterlogged timbers walking against a tide My poet lies unconscious, how to wake her?
Cotton wool runs along all my nerves like a thick sock My wrists no longer run red when the blades kiss them My head no longer hurts when it hits the wall My poet lies unconscious, how to wake her?

February 15

How many nights did I rehearse saying your name? How many times have I rehearsed for 'the announcement'? How many hours has my brain written the story of our hearts? But you don't know the plot, you've barely skimed the pages You are writing a story for strangers' eyes, a carefully sold lie You lace your fingers through hands you'll never feel You keep me in the shadows, tucked behind a velvet curtain I am the footnote of your life — the 'could never be'. Yesterday you bought a card for him, signed it with lies Yesterday he took you to dinner, he told you he loves you Yesterday you posted pictures of the flowers, the chocolates, the wine But I found you on my front door at the hour of lost souls today I know I should have been angry, closed the door in your face I am tired of being your skeleton, a dirty little secret Yet I stay, tethered to a life only lived behind closed curtains Is this love if it's a burden? Is it love if it is a fear? Is this real if you cannot be brave enough to show them me — us? Is this genuine if I'm only ever 'just a friend' — a very close friend? Yes, I will be your gift when their roses are wilting and ribbons fraying

The Last Parcel

I mailed her a picture of us this time, Dancing beneath younger stars With smiles brighter than memory allows No words, just a faded frame from long ago
Maybe she paused, overcome with nostalgia tracing the face she once was fond of Maybe she also plays that night in rewind, like a jazz that never finds release
But perhaps she stared then let it vanish in that drawer she keeps for things unloved Not important enough to frame for the desk Just ghosts humming in the background
